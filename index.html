How Loan Prepayment Works

When you prepay a portion of your loan, the outstanding principal reduces immediately. That reduces the interest charged going forward — which can either lower your monthly EMI or shorten the loan tenure depending on your bank's policy.

  • Immediate principal reduction — less interest accrues each month.
  • Two outcomes — either pay lower EMI or finish earlier.
  • Use the simulator to compare both outcomes and pick the best strategy for you.

Why Prepay? Benefits

  • Save on total interest paid over loan life
  • Become debt-free earlier
  • Reduce financial stress and interest burden

Frequently Asked Questions

What is EMI?

EMI is Equated Monthly Installment — a fixed monthly payment to repay principal and interest over the loan term.

Will prepayment always reduce my EMI?

Not always. Some banks shorten tenure while keeping the EMI same; others reduce EMI. Check your loan terms and use the simulator to model both cases.

Are there penalties for prepayment?

Some lenders charge prepayment fees. Confirm with your bank; the calculator assumes no penalty by default.
